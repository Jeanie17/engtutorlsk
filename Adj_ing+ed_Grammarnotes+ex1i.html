Adjectives Ending with -ing and -ed

A comprehensive guide to describing feelings and the things that cause them

Grammar Presentation and Practice Exercises

Grammar Rule

-ing Adjectives

Describe the thing or situation that causes the feeling.

Example: The movie is interesting.

(The movie causes interest.)

-ed Adjectives

Describe how a person (or animal) feels.

Example: I am interested in the movie.

(I feel interested.)

💡 Quick Tip:

People are -ed, things are -ing!

Example Chart

Cause (-ing) Feeling (-ed)
The game is exciting. The players are excited.
The news is surprising. We are surprised.
The joke is amusing. The audience is amused.
The test is frustrating. The student is frustrated.
The book is boring. The reader is bored.
The movie is frightening. The child is frightened.

Summary

Key Points:

• -ing adjectives → describe things/situations (the cause)

• -ed adjectives → describe people's/animals' feelings (the result)

• Remember: People feel -ed, things are -ing!

Common -ing/-ed Adjective Pairs:

• interesting / interested

• exciting / excited

• boring / bored

• surprising / surprised

• frightening / frightened

• amazing / amazed

• disappointing / disappointed

• amusing / amused

Practice Tips

1. Ask the Right Question

When you're unsure, ask: "Is it describing a thing or a person's feeling?"

2. Create Your Own Examples

Think of situations from your own life. "The math test was challenging. I felt challenged."

3. Read and Notice

When reading English texts, pay attention to how -ing and -ed adjectives are used.

4. Practice with a Friend

Describe your day to a friend using -ing and -ed adjectives.

Final Challenge:

Write one sentence using an -ing adjective and one sentence using an -ed adjective about your favorite hobby.
