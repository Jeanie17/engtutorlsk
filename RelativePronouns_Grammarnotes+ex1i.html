What Are Relative Pronouns?

Relative pronouns connect a clause or phrase to a noun or pronoun.

• They introduce relative clauses (extra information about something).

• Common relative pronouns: who, which, that

Example:

The girl who is reading is Jenny.

(The relative clause "who is reading" tells us more about "the girl")

Another Example:

The dog which is barking belongs to my neighbor.

(The relative clause "which is barking" tells us more about "the dog")

💡 Remember:

Relative pronouns help us combine two simple sentences into one more detailed sentence.

Relative Pronouns Chart

Pronoun Used For Example
who people The teacher who teaches English is kind.
which animals & things The book which is on the table is mine.
that people, animals & things (informal) The dog that barks loudly is next door.

Note: "That" can often replace "who" or "which" in informal English.

Example: "The girl who/that won the race is my friend."

Summary & Tips

Summary:

who
for people
which
for animals and things
that
for people, animals, or things (informal)
📝 Important Tips:

1. The relative pronoun replaces the subject in the second sentence.

2. The relative clause comes right after the noun it describes.

3. You can omit 'that' when it's not the subject (e.g., "The book [that] I read").

4. Practice by combining two simple sentences into one.

Practice Example:

Two sentences: I have a friend. She lives in London.

Combined: I have a friend who lives in London.

Practice in Daily Life

🌟 Ways to practice relative pronouns:

1. Describe people around you: "That's the man who..."

2. Talk about your possessions: "This is the phone which..."

3. Read stories and identify relative pronouns.

4. When writing, try to combine short sentences.

5. Play the "Who/Which am I?" guessing game.

Final Challenge:

Write 3 sentences about your family or friends using relative pronouns.

Example: "My brother, who is 10 years old, loves football."
