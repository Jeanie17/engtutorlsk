📘 Positive / Primary Adjectives
What are positive adjectives?
The base form of an adjective, used to describe a quality without comparison.
Examples: tall, beautiful, expensive, intelligent, fast, good.
✨ Use: "The car is fast." / "She is kind." / "This is an interesting book."

⚖️ Comparative Adjectives
Formation:
• Short adjectives: add -er (tall → taller)
• Adjectives ending in -y: change y → i + er (happy → happier)
• Long adjectives (2+ syllables): more + adjective (beautiful → more beautiful)
• Irregular: good → better, bad → worse, far → farther/further
• Use than for comparison: "She is taller than her brother."
• "as + positive + as" for equality: "He is as fast as me."

👑 Superlative Adjectives
Formation:
• Short adjectives: add -est (tall → tallest)
• -y adjectives: change y → i + est (happy → happiest)
• Long adjectives: most + adjective (beautiful → most beautiful)
• Irregular: good → best, bad → worst, far → farthest/furthest
• Use the before superlative: "Mount Everest is the highest mountain."
• Often followed by "in/of": "She is the smartest in the class."
