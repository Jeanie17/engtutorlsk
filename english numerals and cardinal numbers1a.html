How to Form Numbers (101-999)

Hundred + "and" + Tens/Units

101 = one hundred and one

250 = two hundred and fifty

789 = seven hundred and eighty-nine

Note: The "and" is common in British English and is crucial for clarity.

Key Rule

Say the hundreds first, then the "and", then the rest of the number (tens and units).

Ordinal Numbers: 20th - 99th

The Rule for numbers ending in zero

Change 'y' to 'i' and add -eth

20th = twentieth

30th = thirtieth

40th = fortieth

50th = fiftieth

The Rule for all other numbers

The tens are ordinal, and the units are cardinal.

21st = twenty-first

32nd = thirty-second

57th = fifty-seventh

99th = ninety-ninth

Ordinal Numbers: 100th - 1000th

The Hundreds (100th - 900th)

Rule: Use the cardinal hundred + "and" + last two digits as ordinal.

100th = one hundredth

101st = one hundred and first

242nd = two hundred and forty-second

578th = five hundred and seventy-eighth

One Thousandth

1,000th = one thousandth

How to say a date

"It's the first of January."

"Her birthday is on the twenty-second of May."

"They arrived on the one hundred and first day."
