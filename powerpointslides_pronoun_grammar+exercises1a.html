What Are Pronouns?

Pronouns are words that replace nouns in sentences to avoid repetition and make language more efficient.

Without pronouns: Mary said that Mary would bring Mary's book to Mary's friend.

With pronouns: Mary said that she would bring her book to her friend.

Why Use Pronouns?

  • Make sentences less repetitive
  • Make communication more efficient
  • Help sentences flow better
  • Refer to people or things without naming them repeatedly

Person in Pronouns

Pronouns are categorized by person, which indicates who is speaking, being spoken to, or being spoken about.

First Person

The person speaking

Examples: I, me, my, mine, we, us, our, ours

Second Person

The person being spoken to

Examples: you, your, yours

Third Person

The person being spoken about

Examples: he, him, his, she, her, hers, it, its, they, them, their, theirs

Subject Pronouns

Subject pronouns are used as the subject of a sentence - the person or thing performing the action.

I
First Person Singular
I like coffee.
You
Second Person
You are my friend.
He
Third Person Masculine
He plays soccer.
She
Third Person Feminine
She reads books.
It
Third Person Neutral
It is raining.
We
First Person Plural
We study English.
They
Third Person Plural
They live here.

Object Pronouns

Object pronouns are used as the object of a verb or preposition - the person or thing receiving the action.

me
First Person Singular
She called me.
you
Second Person
I see you.
him
Third Person Masculine
Give it to him.
her
Third Person Feminine
I know her.
it
Third Person Neutral
I bought it.
us
First Person Plural
They invited us.
them
Third Person Plural
We saw them.

Possessive Adjectives

Possessive adjectives show ownership or relationship and come before a noun.

my
First Person Singular
This is my car.
your
Second Person
Is this your book?
his
Third Person Masculine
That is his house.
her
Third Person Feminine
I like her dress.
its
Third Person Neutral
The dog wagged its tail.
our
First Person Plural
We love our school.
their
Third Person Plural
They finished their work.

Key Points to Remember

  • Subject pronouns are the doers of the action
  • Object pronouns receive the action
  • Possessive adjectives come before a noun
  • Possessive pronouns replace a noun
  • Some pronouns like his and its are the same in both possessive forms
