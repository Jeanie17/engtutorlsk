Do & Does in Present Tense

Grammar Rules, Usage, and Practice Exercises

1

When Do We Use Do/Does?

We use do and does as auxiliary (helping) verbs in the Simple Present Tense for:

  • Questions: Do you like coffee?
  • Negative Statements: I do not (don't) like coffee.
  • Short Answers: Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
Do/Does + Subject + Base Verb + ?

Examples:

  • Do you speak English?
  • Does she work here?
  • They don't live in Paris.
  • He doesn't play tennis.
2

The Basic Rule: Do vs. Does

The choice between do and does depends on the subject:

Subject Auxiliary Verb Example
I / You / We / They do Do you like pizza?
He / She / It does Does she like pizza?

Important: When we use does with he/she/it, the main verb returns to its base form (no -s)!

  • She works here. → Does she work here?
  • He plays tennis. → Does he play tennis?
3

Forming Questions with Do/Does

To form questions in Simple Present Tense, we use:

Do/Does + Subject + Base Verb + ?
Subject Question Form Answer
I / you / we / they Do you speak English? Yes, I do. / No, I don't.
he / she / it Does she live here? Yes, she does. / No, she doesn't.

More Examples:

  • Do they like movies?
  • Does he have a car?
  • Do we need to bring anything?
  • Does it rain often here?
4

Forming Negative Statements

To form negative statements in Simple Present Tense, we use:

Subject + do not/does not + Base Verb
Subject Negative Form Contraction
I / you / we / they They do not like coffee. They don't like coffee.
he / she / it She does not work here. She doesn't work here.

Remember: When using "does not/doesn't" with he/she/it, the main verb doesn't get an -s!

  • He works → He doesn't work (NOT doesn't works)
  • She plays → She doesn't play (NOT doesn't plays)

Practice: Do or Does?

Choose the correct option to complete each sentence.

1. ______ you like chocolate?

2. ______ Maria live in Madrid?

3. ______ they speak French?

4. ______ your brother work here?

6

Practice: Complete the Questions

Complete the questions with the correct form of the verb.

1. ______ you ______ (like) classical music?

2. ______ she ______ (work) in an office?

3. ______ they ______ (have) any children?

4. ______ he ______ (play) the guitar?

5. ______ your parents ______ (live) nearby?

Answers:

  1. Do, like
  2. Does, work
  3. Do, have
  4. Does, play
  5. Do, live
7

Practice: Make Negative Sentences

Change these sentences to negative form.

1. I like coffee.

2. She works here.

3. They live in Paris.

4. He plays tennis.

5. We speak French.

Answers:

  1. I don't like coffee.
  2. She doesn't work here.
  3. They don't live in Paris.
  4. He doesn't play tennis.
  5. We don't speak French.
8

Practice: Answer the Questions

Answer these questions using short answers.

1. Do you speak English? (Yes)

2. Does she work here? (No)

3. Do they like pizza? (Yes)

4. Does he play football? (No)

5. Do we have class tomorrow? (Yes)

Answers:

  1. Yes, I do.
  2. No, she doesn't.
  3. Yes, they do.
  4. No, he doesn't.
  5. Yes, we do.
9

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Mistake 1: Adding -s to the main verb with "does"

  • ❌ Does she works here?
  • ✅ Does she work here?

Mistake 2: Using "do" with he/she/it

  • ❌ Do he like coffee?
  • ✅ Does he like coffee?

Mistake 3: Forgetting "do/does" in questions

  • ❌ You like coffee?
  • ✅ Do you like coffee?

Mistake 4: Using "be" instead of "do/does"

  • ❌ Are you like coffee?
  • ✅ Do you like coffee?
10

Summary & Quick Review

  • Use do with I, you, we, they
    • Do you like pizza?
    • They don't live here.
  • Use does with he, she, it
    • Does she work here?
    • He doesn't play tennis.
  • With does, the main verb returns to base form (no -s)
    • She works → Does she work?
  • Use contractions in informal speech:
    • do not → don't
    • does not → doesn't

Final Tip: Practice regularly! The more you use "do" and "does" correctly, the more natural it will become.
